🔥 Challenge Quiz

Q1. The von Neumann architecture's most revolutionary idea was:

✅ Stored-program concept! Before von Neumann, you'd rewire the hardware to change what the computer did. He said — put the program IN memory. One machine, infinite programs. Every computer you've ever used runs on this idea.

Q2. Moore's Law states that transistor count doubles roughly every:

✅ ~2 years! Gordon Moore made this observation in 1965. It's held remarkably well, though the pace has been slowing as we approach physical limits of silicon.

Q3. A program has 1,000,000 instructions and runs on a 2 GHz processor with average CPI of 2. What's the execution time?

✅ 0.001 seconds! T = Ic × CPI × τ = 1,000,000 × 2 × (1/2,000,000,000) = 2,000,000 / 2,000,000,000 = 0.001s. Get comfortable with this calculation — the exam WILL test you on it.

Q4. The Abacus belongs to which era in computing history?

✅ Pre-mechanical! The abacus uses beads to represent numbers — no gears, no electricity, just physical manipulation. This directly answers Q2 in your 2018 exam paper! Pre-mechanical = manual tools (abacus, Napier's bones, finger-counting). Mechanical = gears (Pascaline, Schickard's calculator). Electronic = electricity.
